What is Ruby on Rails?

Ruby on Rails is a powerful and flexible framework that accelerates web application development. With its rich ecosystem, Rails enables developers to build scalable, maintainable, and efficient web applications rapidly.

Why Choose Rails?

Rails emphasizes Convention over Configuration (CoC) and the Don’t Repeat Yourself (DRY) principle, making it a favorite among developers for its simplicity and speed in creating applications.

The Rails Philosophy

Ruby on Rails is built on the philosophy of making web development fun and productive. It encourages developers to write beautiful code by providing a clean and concise framework that handles the heavy lifting, allowing you to focus on what makes your application unique.

Rails in Action

Rails powers many popular websites and applications, demonstrating its flexibility and robustness. Companies like GitHub, Shopify, and Airbnb rely on Rails to handle their extensive and dynamic web needs.

Get Started with Rails

Interested in learning more about Ruby on Rails? Whether you're a beginner or an experienced developer, Rails offers the tools and community support you need to succeed.

Rails Community

The Ruby on Rails community is one of its strongest assets. With a wealth of tutorials, guides, and open-source contributions, developers have access to a vast pool of knowledge and resources. This collaborative environment fosters innovation and continuous improvement.

About Ruby on Rails

Ruby on Rails was created in 2004 by David Heinemeier Hansson and has since become one of the most popular frameworks for building web applications. It’s open-source and supported by a large community of developers.
